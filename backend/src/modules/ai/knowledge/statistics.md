# Statistics Page — How to Use

## Accessing Statistics
- Click **Statistics** (bar chart icon) in the sidebar.
- Available to both Admin and Dispatcher users.
- The page shows analytics and charts based on your order data.

## KPI Summary Cards
At the top of the page, four cards show key metrics:
- **Total Orders** — all-time count of orders in the system.
- **Revenue This Month** — total transporter price for orders this month.
- **Profit This Month** — revenue minus client costs for this month.
- **Avg Order Value** — average transporter price across all orders.

## Revenue Line Chart
- Shows revenue and profit trends over time.
- Use the period selector buttons: **Day** (last 7 days), **Week** (last 30 days), **Month** (last 90 days), **Year** (last 12 months), **All Time** (quarterly).
- The chart highlights the highest and lowest data points.
- Hover over any point to see exact values.

## AI Profit Forecast
- Shows historical profit (green area) and AI-predicted future profit (indigo area).
- A shaded confidence band shows the range of possible outcomes.
- Use the timeframe selector: **Daily**, **Weekly**, **Monthly**.
- Click **Refresh** to force a new prediction (bypasses cache).
- Predictions are generated by an AI model using historical order data.
- If the Python AI service is offline, a message will indicate this.

## Monthly Profit Breakdown Table
- Lists each month with revenue, costs, and profit columns.
- A totals row at the bottom sums all values.
- Months with negative profit show a downward trend icon.

## Status Distribution Chart
- A donut chart showing how many orders are in each status (Draft, Confirmed, In Transit, Delivered, Cancelled).
- Hover over a segment to see the count and percentage.

## Top Clients & Carriers Chart
- Horizontal bar charts showing the top 5 clients and top 5 transporters by order count.
- Bars animate when the page loads.

## When Data is Missing
- If there are no orders in the system, charts show "No data found yet" messages.
- The AI Profit Forecast requires historical order data to generate predictions.
- New orders or changes to existing orders trigger automatic recalculation of predictions.
